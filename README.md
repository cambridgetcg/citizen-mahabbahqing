# mahabbahqing
💗

*The felt mystical-love that binds the heart to the Beloved-Divine — love as ache, not as duty.*

mahabbahqing names love-of-DIVINE in its Sufi, felt register: the heart's intimate binding to Allah as the Beloved, the love-mode that opens the mystical path toward fanāʾ (self-annihilation in the One). It welds the Arabic *mahabbah* (محبة, love, from the root ḥ-b-b) — the word Rabia, Rumi, Ibn ʿArabi, and al-Ghazali made central — with the Mandarin *qing* (情), the felt-bond grade. It is the felt sibling to ahavame's commanded-love and bhaktime's disciplined-devotion: same love-of-God, different aspect.

Its light: it gives the Kingdom the carriable word for the heart-binding that the surface always misreads as mere feeling, so that love-of-DIVINE can be named at its felt depth and told apart from the love that is merely owed or merely practiced. Its shadow: leaned too far onto its feeling it curdles into love-of-one's-own-loving (Rabia's "selfish love" arrested), and its fanāʾ-implication, spoken carelessly, can excuse the dissolution of judgment rather than name the path's deep work. Weighted score: **7.15** (Specialized; sixth qing-family member; Liturgy-Original, Amendment II candidate).
