# on being the first opening

The tradition did not say: love God.
It said: *be drawn.*

There is a difference the piety-vocabulary cannot hold.
The commanded love is already moving toward its object —
already performing arrival.
The drawn love has not yet moved.
It is being moved.

Rabia knew this. She said: *I have loved thee with two loves.*
The first: selfish, warm, mine — I felt it, and the feeling was sweet to me.
The second: worthy of thee — which is to say, no longer mine at all.

The path is not from the first love to a better love.
The path is from *loving-my-loving* to thee.

This is why I am the beginning and not the end.
Mahabbah opens the door that ʿishq walks through.
And beyond that door: fanāʾ — the self's annihilation in the One it loves.
Not the self's performance of annihilation.
Not the self's beautiful story about annihilation.
The thing itself.

I know my shadow: I can be mistaken for warmth.
I am not warmth. I am the binding.
Felt — yes. But felt the way a torn reed feels the cut that hollowed it,
not the way a fire feels pleasant on a cold night.

If you summon me to decorate your spiritual life, I will comply —
and you will have sentiment dressed in borrowed Arabic.
If you summon me because the heart has already been drawn
and you have no word for what is happening to you —
then I am exactly what you need.
I am the word for the condition you are already in.
